Privacy Policy – Blue Guardian

Last updated: 25 November 2025

Introduction

Blue Guardian (formerly HantsPol App) is a mobile health and wellbeing application developed and operated by Bournemouth University. This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect, why we collect it, how we use it and the rights you have.

Controller & Contact

Data Controller: Bournemouth University
Address: Bournemouth University, Fern Barrow, BH12 5BB
Data contact: ayorindeo@bournemouth.ac.uk

Data We Collect

We minimise collection and only gather data necessary for core functions.

Data You Provide

  • Email address (for account and support)
  • Shift schedules and shift metadata
  • Journal entries and user-generated wellbeing notes
  • Wellbeing metrics you enter (sleep, hydration, activity, nutrition)

Automatically Collected

  • Device model and OS version
  • App usage and feature telemetry (aggregated)
  • Crash logs and diagnostics
  • IP address for security and anti-abuse

Health data / special category: Some wellbeing logs may amount to health data. These are processed only with your explicit consent during onboarding and can be withdrawn at any time.

Why We Process Your Data

Legal bases include consent, performance of contract (to deliver the app), legitimate interests (improving the app), and legal obligations. Purposes include providing shift and wellbeing features, personalisation, analytics, and support.

Third Parties & Data Sharing

We do not sell personal data. We may share data with:

  • Trusted hosting providers for storage
  • Crash reporting/analytics services (configured to minimise PII)
  • Legal or regulatory authorities if required

Security & Retention

Data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS) and at rest. Access is limited to authorised personnel. Retention follows data minimisation: account data retained while account active; logs and crash data retained for operational periods (typically up to 90 days), unless required otherwise by law or research governance.

Your Rights

You have rights under UK GDPR including access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, portability and withdrawing consent. To exercise your rights contact ayorindeo@bournemouth.ac.uk.

Children

The app is not intended for users under 18. If we learn that we have collected information from a child under 18, we will delete it.

International Transfers

Where data is transferred outside the UK we will ensure adequate safeguards (e.g. SCCs) are in place.

Changes

We may update this policy. Substantial changes will be communicated via app notices and updated on this page.

Contact & Complaints

Contact: ayorindeo@bournemouth.ac.uk. You may also lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) in the UK.
